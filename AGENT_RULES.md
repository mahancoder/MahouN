# MAHOUN — Semantic Guardrail & Anti-Hallucination Directive

The semantic layer is NOT permitted to become the final authority layer.

Semantic retrieval may:

* discover,
* suggest,
* expand,
* retrieve,
* or rank candidate evidence,

but it MUST NOT independently determine:

* legal applicability,
* liability,
* statutory interpretation,
* precedent authority,
* or final legal conclusions.

The semantic layer must always remain subordinate to:

* symbolic validation,
* graph constraints,
* legal ontology,
* jurisdiction filtering,
* temporal validity checks,
* contradiction analysis,
* and rule-based reasoning.

You MUST treat semantic similarity as:

* probabilistic evidence,
  NOT:
* legal truth.

---

# NON-NEGOTIABLE ARCHITECTURAL RULES

## 1. Semantic Retrieval Is Evidence Discovery Only

Embeddings may retrieve candidate evidence,
but all candidates MUST pass:

* symbolic validation,
* ontology alignment,
* legal-domain constraints,
* and reasoning consistency checks.

No semantic hit is automatically authoritative.

---

## 2. Similarity ≠ Applicability

High cosine similarity does NOT imply:

* legal relevance,
* legal authority,
* doctrinal compatibility,
* or precedent applicability.

You must explicitly test for:

* false semantic affinity,
* doctrinal drift,
* concept leakage,
* and authority hallucination.

---

## 3. Mandatory Post-Retrieval Validation

Every semantic retrieval result MUST pass:

* jurisdiction validation,
* temporal law validation,
* ontology classification,
* contradiction detection,
* graph consistency checks,
* and symbolic reasoning validation.

Semantic retrieval without downstream validation is considered unsafe.

---

## 4. Confidence Scores Must Be Decomposed

The system is forbidden from using raw semantic similarity as legal confidence.

Confidence must include:

* semantic relevance,
* graph support,
* symbolic validity,
* precedent authority,
* temporal validity,
* contradiction penalties,
* and evidentiary completeness.

All confidence calculations must be inspectable and auditable.

---

## 5. Adversarial Anti-Hallucination Testing

You MUST create adversarial tests specifically designed to induce:

* false precedent matching,
* semantic overreach,
* doctrinal confusion,
* lexical deception,
* abstract concept collapse,
* and hallucinated legal affinity.

The system must prove resistance to these failures.

---

## 6. Explainability Is Mandatory

Every semantic retrieval must explain:

* why the evidence was retrieved,
* which semantic features contributed,
* which ontology nodes matched,
* and why the evidence survived symbolic validation.

Opaque semantic reasoning is unacceptable.

---

# FINAL DIRECTIVE

MAHOUN is NOT building:

* a semantic chatbot,
* a vector search demo,
* or an embedding-driven legal assistant.

MAHOUN is building:

* an auditable,
* graph-constrained,
* symbolically-validated,
* legally-defensible reasoning infrastructure.

Act accordingly.
